Copyright


Copyright is a standard developed to protect your IP. When you create a piece of media copyright is automatically applied for your lifetime plus 50 years. Copyright protects your work from other individuals by making sure that they cannot use your work as their own. Copyright exists and applies in most countries this means that copyright is under international law. (Copyright, n.d.)

Website publishing

When publishing websites in New Zealand you must follow a set of rules that have been created by the New Zealand government to regulate how websites are published. When it comes to copyright when publishing a website in New Zealand there is a subset of rules that must be followed, in New Zealand, you must have a general copyright statement, this statement must follow the rules set out on the government website. (DITAL.GOVT.NZ, 2020)

Creative Commons Licensing

Creative Commons licensing is a standardized system of how media is shared between people for their creative work. Creative Commons relies on 6 principles, each principle gives the creator certain levels of freedom in terms of what they can use the media for.
When a creator creates a piece of media creative commons automatically apply, this is called all rights reserved. This means that the work cannot be changed and purely belongs to the creator of the price of media. But let’s say the creator wants to share their work so people can reuse, remix and remake their work, to do this they can apply the different laws of creative commons to their work giving people levels of freedom to do what want to your work. (Creative Common, n.d.)
In international law each country has its own interpretation of the law, this is called a “port” of creative commons as compatibility may not be possible in every country, recently there is a generic law that is used by most countries and does not require a port. New Zealand does have a port of the creative commons license. (Wikipeda , 2017)

Privacy Act 1993

When publishing a website, you follow the privacy act of 1993, this act is designed to protect an individual’s privacy. This act follows the principals set out by the New Zealand government, if this act is broken the creator could end up in major legal issues. When publishing your website, you must take the privacy of individuals into account. (Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet (DPMC), 2017)
